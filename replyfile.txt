Based on the excerpts provided, Gandhi strongly opposed the partition of India. He believed in the innate goodness of human nature and condemned the communalism that had intensified due to separate electorates. He argued that a Constituent Assembly was necessary to resolve the deadlock and that nonviolence was the only way to achieve real freedom for India. He also mentioned that if the Muslim vote went in favor of partition, it would lead to either multiple partitions or civil war. Overall, Gandhi believed that a united India was the best solution and partition should be avoided.
