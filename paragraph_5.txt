 Gandhi said he could not bring himself to accept the proposal of a 25 per cent reduction. Mr. Reid inquired whether the alternative to a settlement by agreement was the suggestion of a tribunal. Mr. Gandhi said that if the Committee could not come to an agreement, he would be prepared to accept the suggestion of a tribunal, but he asked whether the Committee could not recommend a higher reduction. The President said that he thought the Committee had got the highest possible figures out of the planters and he was not prepared to agree to higher figure. He could not imagine that any raiyats would ever regret having accepted this proposal; the only raiyats who might be discontented were the few raiyats who still grew indigo.
